## whoami

I'm a full-stack engineer who likes to ship. I'm not "passionate about clean code," I just like building things that work and putting them in front of people.

Most of what I build comes back to one thing: I'm obsessed with systems. Markets, history, strategy games, they're all the same kind of puzzle to me. I backtest trading ideas at 2am, read about why empires fall apart, and lose whole weekends to grand-strategy sims. Taking a system apart until it makes sense is just what I do, whether that's a 1500s trade route or a React render cycle.

A job search is a system too, which is most of why I'm building what I'm building.

<br/>

## Currently building

### [Trekky.app](https://trekky.app): the job-search operating system for software engineers

*Track. Steady. Land.*

The tool I wish I'd had. One calm pipeline that runs the whole search, instead of a spreadsheet slowly dying across fifteen browser tabs. Track every role, prep for every interview, and follow up before the opportunity goes cold.

- Drag every application through its stages, with stack, seniority, comp, and close date on the card
- Paste a LinkedIn or Seek link and it pulls the role, comp, and stack for you, no data entry
- AI that reads the real job description and your resume, then tailors the resume, drafts the cover letter, and drills the interview questions you'll actually get
- Salary benchmarks, company research, offer comparison, and Google Calendar sync, run by an assistant that knows your pipeline instead of a blank ChatGPT tab

Live and free during beta, built in the open. Private by default, no recruiter marketplace.

`Next.js 16` · `React 19` · `TypeScript` · `MongoDB` · `Gemini` · `Stripe` · `Vercel`

- **If I can't explain how something works, I don't understand it yet.**
- **Boring tech for the foundation, interesting tech where it counts.**
